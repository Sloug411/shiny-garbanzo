![[pinnatecompound.png]]

Above is a *Pinnately* Compound leaf. It can't really be seen in this picture, but you should know this whole thing only has one [[Axillary Bud]] (Wow!). This means that all those leaves you see right there are actually leaflets of one big leaf, making it a compound leaf (Double Wow!). In a simple leaf there's a bud at the base of it's single leaflet, but on compound leaves there's a bud along the twig near where the [[Petiole|stalk]] attaches, but not directly on the stalk. This picture is *Pinnately* compound, which means it has leaflets arranged on opposite sides of an rachis (the central axis seen above).

![[palmatecompound.png|432]]

The picture above is a *Palmately* Compound leaf. In contrast to the *Pinnately* Compound leaf, all the leaflets attach at a common point. There is still only one bud here.
