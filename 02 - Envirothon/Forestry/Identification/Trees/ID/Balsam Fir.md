**Characteristics**: The leaves on a Balsam Fir (AKA Abies Balsamea) are evergreen and about an inch long. They spread almost at right angles in two rows on hairy twigs. The leaves are a shiny, dark green above, with two whitish bands beneath. The twigs should be smooth or nearly smooth. The cones of a Balsam Fir are about 2-3 inches long, cylindrical, green tinged & dark purple, and upright on the topmost twigs. The cones have scales that are finely hairy, and bracts that are mostly short and hidden. The [[Trunk|bark]] is brown, thin, smooth, and has so many resin blisters it looks scaly.

![[firleaves.png|450]]![[firtree.png]]

**FYI**: Balsam Fir is the ==only fir native to the Northeast.== Its foliage is pretty aromatic. It is a ==major [[Pulpwood|pulpwood]] species.== It is used alternatively for interior knotty pine paneling, Christmas trees, and wreaths. It's occasionally used in pillows cause the foliage smells so sweet. Deer browse the Fir in winter.

![[firtwig.png]]![[firbark.png]]

**Identification Guide**: When identifying this tree you should first notice that the leaves are [[Leaf Types|needles]]. Next you should notice how the leaves are singular on the [[Twigs|twig]], showing that it is a [[Simple Leaf|simple leaf]]. Afterwards you should notice how the needles are flat and blunt, and that they ==do not have stalks.== Those characteristics are the key ways one can identify a Fir.
