**Characteristics**: The leaves of an Eastern Hemlock (AKA Tsuga Canadensis) are very ==flat, half an inch long==, dark green and glossy on the top, and light green with ==two white lines on the underside.== They are also evergreen. The twigs are very slender, but tough, and a yellowish-brown to a grayish-brown. The buds are egg-shaped and a reddish brown. The [[Fruit|fruit]] of an Eastern Hemlock are ==egg-shaped== and hang from the tips of twigs. The [[Trunk|bark]] is flaky on younger trees. It is colored a ==gray-brown to red-brown, and it is thick and grooved when older.== The Eastern Hemlock lives for a long time naturally.

![[hemlockcones.png|649]]

**FYI**: Eastern Hemlock is the ==OFFICIAL STATE TREE== (IMPORTANT INFO!!!) of Pennsylvania. It is usually used for construction, and tannic acid for tanning leather. Eastern Hemlock is found in cool, moist woods in Pennsylvania. [[Ruffed Grouse|ruffed grouse]], wild turkey, and songbirds use this tree for food and shelter. Deer like it a lot when snow makes other food sources scarce. The Hemlock Woody Adelgid is ==killing of most of the trees in the state,== and it is nearly impossible to stop.

![[easternhemlock2.png|639]]

**Identification Guide**: The main thing to consider when identifying Eastern Hemlock is the leaves. One must first notice how the leaves are [[Leaf Types|needles]]. See that unlike some trees with needles, the needles are very flat and blunt. The next to notice is how there is a [[Axillary Bud|bud]] for every leaflet, meaning it is a tree that has [[Simple Leaf|simple leaves]]. The final thing to notice is that the [[Petiole|petiole]] is extremely small. If you see all of those things, the tree is almost certainly a Hemlock.
