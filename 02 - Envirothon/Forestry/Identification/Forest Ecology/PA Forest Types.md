#Forestry #Study 

Three major factors determines the type of forest that develops in PA, temperature, rainfall, and topography.

![[foresttypes.png]]

**Northern Hardwood Forests**: These forests occupies the northern third of PA, and extends south at high elevations along the [[Allegheny Front]]. This forest type is characterized by a mixture of [[Hardwoods]] and [[Conifers]]. The Northern Hardwood Forest usually contains [[Beech]], [[Birch]], [[Sugar Maple]], [[Eastern Hemlock]], and [[White Pine]] in the canopy.

**Oak Forests**: These two forest types dominate the southern two-thirds of PA. They include red oak-mixed [[Hardwoods|hardwood]] type trees on lower slopes where [[Red Oak|red]] and [[White Oak|white]] oaks occur mixed with [[Tuliptree]], [[Red Maple]], and [[Hickory|Hickories]]. These forests often have a dense layer of shrubs like the [[Mountain Laurel]] and [[Black Huckleberry]]. Before 1910, [[American Chestnut]] was an important part of PA's dry oak forests, but after the introduction of Chestnut Blight in NYC, it has become very minor.

**Beech-Maple Forests**: These forests are at the western end of PA, around the Great Lakes.

**Mixed Mesophytic Forests**: These forests reach their greatest development in the Smoky Mountains that just barely reach southern PA.
