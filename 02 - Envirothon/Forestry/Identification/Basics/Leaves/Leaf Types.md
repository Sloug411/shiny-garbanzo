#Forestry #Study 

There are three main leaf types, scale-like, broad, and needles.

![[scaleleaf.png|225]]

The *Scale-like* leaf type is defined by its thin, flat leaves and how it presses closely to the branch. Classic examples of this is the [[Aborvitae]] and [[Northern White-Cedar]]

![[broadleaf.png]]

The *Broad and Flat* leaf type is... broad and flat (Duh). This is a common characteristic of many [[Deciduous]] species.

![[needleleaf.png]]

The *Needle* leaf type is not necessarily spikey as one might think. They can be spikey, eg. [[Pines]] and [[Spruces]], but they can also be more blunt like the [[Eastern Hemlock]].
