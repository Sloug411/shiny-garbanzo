#Forestry #Study 

There are three main leaf arrangements, alternate, opposite, and whorled.

![[altleaves.png]]

The *Alternate* leaf arrangement is when the petioles are staggered along the stem. The [[Axillary Bud|bud]] is at the junction of the twig and Petiole. After the leaves fall the bud will remain.

![[opleaves.png|601]]

The *Opposite* leaf arrangement is when two leaves attach to the stem directly across from each other. The buds are at the junction of the leaf and twig. In P.A. trees with this configuration are often either Maple, Ash, or Dogwood, which can be shortened to M.A.D.

![[whorledleaves.png|160]]

The *Whorled* leaf arrangement is when *three* or more leaves are attached at the same point along the stem. These are mainly [[Conifers]], with a few exceptions.
