**Description**: "[[Julian Coseley]]'s epistolary treatise on 'what may come after', addressed to his once-friend [[Claude Hersault]], the mystic and antiquarian."

**Study**: "By the time he wrote these letters, Coseley was identified with the oneiropolitical '[[Worms]]' movement, and Hersault with the '[[Birds]]' - meaning roughly that Hersault held the order of the Hours to be desirable, and Coseley held it to be oppressive."

**Mastery**: "Many of Coseley's arguments about consequence and finality are couched in erudite and practical alchemical terms. Others appear to be thinly-veiled threats directed at Hersault. Some are both."
