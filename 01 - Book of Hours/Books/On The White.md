#Winter #Unanalyzed 

![[onthewhite.png]]
**Description**: "[[Solomon Husher]] writes, perhaps allegorically, of [[Winter]], and it's long doomed romance with the [[Glory|Sun]]. The Epigraph is 'Sunset at Noon'"

**Study**: "On closer investigation, the work seems to be a theory of aesthetics, or perhaps a set of warnings about the dangers of painting."

**Mastery**: "Husher writes distractedly of his hatred of colours and his yearning for death. He hints at a [[Great Work]] he has envisaged, or begun, where the 'palest of paintings' will enthrall the world. He returns again and again to certain compelling phrases which he claims are the 'secret words of Winter'."

**Analysis**: The most interesting thing to discuss about On The White is what Husher means by the "Sun," as it can reveal a lot about the nature of Winter. He can mean three things, the [[Glory]], [[The Sun-in-Splendour]], or the [[Lantern]] aspect. The least likely is [[The Sun-in-Splendour]] theory, as that [[Hours|Hour]] is known to be in a relationship [[The Forge of Days]].
